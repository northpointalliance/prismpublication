## What Plain does that Prism should steal

Plain broke through a crowded B2B SaaS market (support tickets) by doing five things nobody else was doing in their category:

1. **Named the enemy, not the competitor.** They didn't attack Zendesk or Intercom by name. They named the *feeling* — "duct tape, rigid workflows, lock-in." Anyone who has used a bad support tool felt that in their gut.

2. **Called themselves infrastructure, not software.** "AI support stack" and "composable infrastructure" signal depth without buzzwords. It tells technical buyers: this is serious.

3. **Made the buyer feel smart.** "We'll make it easy for you. Here's why, and when, we win." Honest framing — they tell you when *not* to use them. That builds more trust than a features grid.

4. **Specific time claims.** "29 minutes and 59 seconds." Not "get started fast." Real numbers make abstract promises physical.

5. **Humanity in the process, not just the product.** "When you want to enjoy the buying process. Seriously." Nobody else says that. It disarms.

## The one positioning sentence Prism is missing

Plain has: *"AI support infrastructure that enables B2B teams to build anything"*

Prism needs an equivalent. Draft options:

1. "The monetization layer for AI chat publishers."
2. "Contextual partner integrations for AI conversations — served at the moment of stated intent."
3. ~~"The only ad infrastructure built for the inside of a conversation."~~ **Retired 2026-07-03 — false.** Kontext (SSP partner, named publisher case study) is a direct competitor already ahead on proof points. OpenAI launched ChatGPT ads Feb 9, 2026. Amazon is exploring third-party chatbot ad infrastructure. Do not claim "only" or "nobody else" anywhere.

**Working replacement:** something that claims the long-tail publisher niche honestly instead of category uniqueness — e.g. "Built for the AI apps too small for the platforms and too specific for the generic ad networks." Needs testing before it goes anywhere external; not yet validated the way #3 falsely was.
